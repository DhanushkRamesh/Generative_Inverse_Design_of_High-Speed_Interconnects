This paper also focus on solving the bottlenecks by the traditional EM solvers as they are time-consuming and need multiple iterative trail-and error runs to find the exact physical geometries. 

The authors here propose a novel approach of Random Parameter Variation and using small datasets - so this is a data collection method called Random Parameter Variation - where they randomly varied physical parameters like trace width, substrate height, etc., to perform the baseline EM simulations. By monitoring the Mean Squared Error (MSE) against the training size they proved they only needed 85 training samples to achieve 95% accuracy. 

They used two part architecture - supervised ML models - to handle the complete design cycle. 

Model 1: Inverse model - Synthesis:  This model uses Artificial Neural Network to predict the required physical properties based on the desired electrical specifications. 

Model 2: Forward Model - They used an Ensemble model combining K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) and Decision Tree (DT) regressors. The final output is weighed average of prediction from both algorithms.
