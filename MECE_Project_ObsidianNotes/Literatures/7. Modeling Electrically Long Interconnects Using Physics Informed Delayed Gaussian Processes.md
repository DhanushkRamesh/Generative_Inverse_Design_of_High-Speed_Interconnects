This paper deals with highly oscillating S-parameters found in electronically long interconnects. So in standard neural networks and support vector machines - they require massive amount of data and are highly prone to overfitting. Alternatively Gaussian Processes (GP) is used because they are probabilistic, and handles small datasets well. Due to propagation delay in electronically long interconnects, the S-parameters oscillate widely and completely fail to accurately model these oscillating freq. responses.

The authors propose delayed Gaussian Processes to solve this - they developed a physics-informed kernel that naturally incorporates periodic components representing those physical propagation delay.
